GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT (GDP) In Malaysia

Gross Domestic Product

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is one of the most widely used indicators of a country's economic performance. This estimates and captures the monetary value being created within a country's borders over a specific period. GDP is measured through the economy, with breakdowns available by economic sector and expenditure category. As a key data release monitored by policy makers, private sector, and international community. High GDP often signals a stronger economy, while fluctuations can reveal trends in growth or contraction. Nevertheless GDP alone do not account for income inequality, environmental impacts, or quality of life.

Malaysia's GDP is a key indicator of its economic performance, where malaysia has very diverse economy which is driven by key sectors like manufacturing, services and natural resources. Malaysia has maintained steady econoy growth, supported by its geographical position in Southeast Asia (SEA) and its role of a global exporter of electronics, palm oil, oil, and gas products.
